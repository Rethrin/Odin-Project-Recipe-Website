Lasagna: The Decadent and Meaty Delight

Picture this: layers upon layers of tender lasagna noodles, smothered in rich tomato sauce and melted mozzarella cheese, with each bite oozing with savory goodness. The aroma of fragrant herbs and spices fills the air, as you take a forkful of the perfectly seasoned ground beef and ricotta cheese filling, perfectly balanced with just the right amount of tanginess and creaminess. Topped off with a golden brown, crispy crust, this lasagna is the ultimate comfort food that will leave your taste buds craving more. This classic Italian dish is a family favorite that never fails to impress, and is sure to become a staple in your recipe repertoire. So grab a glass of red wine, sit back, and enjoy a heaping plate of this mouthwatering lasagna.

Ingredients Required:

  • 1 lb. lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb. ground beef
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28 oz.) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (6 oz.) can tomato paste
  • 2 tsp. dried basil
  • 2 tsp. dried oregano
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1/2 tsp. black pepper
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 large eggs
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

The Process:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375Â°F.
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, rinse them in cold water and set them aside.
  3. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, cook the ground beef until it is browned and cooked through. Drain any excess fat and add the onion and garlic to the skillet. Cook until the onion is translucent.
  4. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir to combine and simmer the sauce for 15-20 minutes.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix the ricotta cheese, 1 cup of the shredded mozzarella cheese, 1/2 cup of the grated Parmesan cheese, and the eggs until well combined.
  6. To assemble the lasagna, spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Top with a layer of lasagna noodles, followed by a layer of the ricotta cheese mixture. Repeat these layers until all ingredients are used up, ending with a layer of meat sauce on top.
  7. Sprinkle the remaining shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan cheese on top of the lasagna.
  8. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly and the lasagna is heated through.
  9. Allow the lasagna to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley and enjoy!

    10. This recipe makes approximately 8-10 servings, so it's perfect for feeding a crowd. Enjoy!
